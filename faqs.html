Air Conditioning Systems

What size air conditioner do I need?

The size of air conditioner you need depends on several factors, including the square footage of the space, ceiling height, insulation quality, window size and orientation, and local climate. As a general rule, you need approximately 20 BTU per square foot of living space. However, for an accurate assessment, we recommend scheduling a site survey with our professionals who can perform proper load calculations to determine the ideal size for your specific needs.

How often should I service my air conditioner?

We recommend having your air conditioning system professionally serviced at least once a year, preferably before the start of the cooling season. Regular maintenance ensures optimal performance, extends the lifespan of your system, prevents unexpected breakdowns, and maintains energy efficiency. For systems that run year-round or in areas with extreme conditions, semi-annual maintenance (twice a year) is advisable. Additionally, air filters should be checked monthly and cleaned or replaced as needed.

How does the air conditioning system work?

Air conditioning systems work on the principle of heat transfer. The system extracts heat from your indoor air and releases it outside, resulting in cooler indoor temperatures. This process involves several key components:

  • The evaporator coil absorbs heat from indoor air
  • Refrigerant circulates through the system, carrying heat from inside to outside
  • The compressor pressurizes the refrigerant
  • The condenser coil releases heat outside
  • The expansion valve regulates refrigerant flow
  • The blower fan circulates air through the system

This cycle repeats continuously until the desired temperature is reached.

What settings do I need to choose so that the room will cool faster?

To cool a room faster, consider these settings and practices:

  • Set the temperature to your desired comfort level rather than to an extremely low temperature (the system will cool at the same rate regardless)
  • Use the "Cool" mode rather than "Auto" for more continuous cooling
  • Set the fan speed to "High" for maximum air circulation
  • Ensure all vents and registers are open and unobstructed
  • Close blinds or curtains to block heat from sunlight
  • Use ceiling fans in conjunction with your AC to help distribute cool air

Remember that extremely low temperature settings won't cool your room faster but will cause your system to run longer, using more energy.

Is this model power efficient?

All AIR BREEZE models are designed with energy efficiency as a priority. The energy efficiency of air conditioners is typically measured by their SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) rating. The higher the SEER rating, the more energy-efficient the unit. Our current models range from SEER 16 to SEER 24, well above the minimum standard requirements.

For specific information about a particular model, we recommend discussing your needs with our sales team who can provide detailed efficiency metrics and help you calculate potential energy savings based on your usage patterns and local utility rates. Many of our models also qualify for energy rebates and tax incentives due to their high efficiency ratings.
